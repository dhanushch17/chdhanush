About Me

How I Started

I took Computer Science as my Bachelor's without knowing how weird and scary (yeah it's a nightmare even today) it can be. After days of searching for an alternate career path, Design took my interest and it has been two years, I never once regretted my decision.

I started with Graphic Design and when I was doing my internship, my manager coined the word UX for the first time to me. He told "Experience matters !!". My career took a sharp turn ever since, I looked up UX everywhere, learned it, practiced it and used it to design real world products. I can now proudly call myself a User Experience Designer.

My Philosophy

Focusing on creating experiences that accommodate user's interactions and responses, rather than trying to create new and different experiences that users can't relate to. After all, who has the time in their busy life to be thinking about how they should use an app or a website?

Always keep in mind that 'Designers are not users' and we shouldn't rely on our assumptions of what the user wants or needs. A really good design must emphasizes the usefulness of a product and should equally be soothing for eyes, while disregarding anything that could possibly detract from it.
